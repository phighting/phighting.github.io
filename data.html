Semi-Supervised Music Emotion Recognition with Social Tagging

2011/12-2012/4

This work solves the severe lacking of labeled data problem in music emotion recognition. We modeled this problem as a regression problem and incorporated graph-based semi-supervised learning to make use of content information of unlabeled data. We also designed a denoising scheme to refine the noisy tags, which improve the performance subsequently.

Advisor

Prof. Andrew Horner, Prof. Qiang Yang

Multiple Instance Learning for Horror Detection

2009/12-2011/4

This project was proposed by me. We investigated horror scene detection as a special Multiple Instance Learning problem, and proposed methods to fuse the horror scene’s multiple features. Also, we developed a website to collect people’s scores on horror scenes and the score ranking information was adapted to the labeling process. The website attracted over 6000 visiting at the first day placed on the Internet and was SJTU 2nd hottest topic on 2010-5-31. This project has been awarded Excellent Project in The 3rd Shanghai College Students Innovation Program.
